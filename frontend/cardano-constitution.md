# Cardano Constitution

## Preamble

This is a placeholder constitution file for local development. In production, this file will be fetched from the GitHub repository.

## Article I - Governance Principles

### Section 1 - Decentralization
The Cardano blockchain shall operate as a decentralized network governed by its community of stakeholders.

### Section 2 - Transparency
All governance decisions and proposals shall be publicly accessible and verifiable on-chain.

### Section 3 - Participation
Every ADA holder shall have the right to participate in the governance process proportional to their stake.

## Article II - Amendment Process

### Section 1 - Constitutional Amendments
Constitutional amendments may be proposed through the Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP) process.

### Section 2 - Deliberation Period
All proposals shall undergo a mandatory 30-day deliberation period for community review and discussion.

### Section 3 - Ratification
Amendments shall be ratified through on-chain voting by the Cardano stakeholder community.

## Article III - Institutional Framework

### Section 1 - Constitutional Council
A Constitutional Council shall be established to review proposals for constitutional compliance.

### Section 2 - Technical Review
All technical changes shall undergo peer review by qualified community members.

## Article IV - Rights and Responsibilities

### Section 1 - Stakeholder Rights
Stakeholders have the right to:
- Propose amendments
- Vote on proposals
- Participate in deliberations
- Access all governance records

### Section 2 - Responsibilities
All participants shall:
- Act in good faith
- Respect the deliberation process
- Provide constructive feedback
- Maintain civility in discussions
