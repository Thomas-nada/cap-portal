# CAP Editors: Role, Scope and Guidelines

---

## Mission & Purpose

CAP Editors serve as the stewards of the CAP (Cardano Amendment Proposal) process. Their objective is to ensure the workflow remains consistent, transparent, and high-quality.

Editors are **facilitators, not decision-makers**. They maintain the integrity of the process — ensuring that submissions are structured, auditable, and ready for public review — without ever encroaching upon the author's ownership of a proposal's substance. Editors make suggestions; authors decide whether to accept or reject them. This is shown transparently in the process, and it is up to the community to decide whether a final proposal — with or without editor suggestions — is acceptable.

Recognising that, given the decentralised nature of Cardano, authors may at any time withdraw their proposal from the CAP process and submit it on-chain directly as a governance action, this document sets out the scope of the editors' role within the CAP.

---

## Key Responsibilities

The primary duties of CAP Editors include:

**Initial Reviews** — Conducting an initial review of a CAP within two weeks of its submission. Editors will examine:
- Constitutionality of the proposal — will the proposal itself be constitutional? Is it in accordance with the tenets and other provisions?
- Whether the proposal requires changes to other parts of the Constitution.
- Whether transitional provisions are needed to ensure live governance actions are not unintentionally blocked.
- Grammatical or other editorial errors in the proposed change.
- Any other concerns raised by the nature of the proposal.

**Providing Feedback** — Reviewing submitted proposals and offering feedback to improve them in line with the Constitution and the author's original purpose.

**Suggesting Edits** — Recommending precise edits when a proposal lacks clarity. Editors must not rewrite the intent or substance without explicit author authorisation.

**Direct Editing** — Performing direct edits only when explicitly authorised by the author.

**Organising Meetings** — Organising regular public meetings between editors and authors to discuss ongoing CAPs and CISs.

**Applying Rules Consistently** — Applying process rules consistently to all submitters and organising discussion spaces to stay on-topic and neutral.

---

## Operational Guidelines: Do's and Don'ts

| Responsibility | CAP Editors DO | CAP Editors DO NOT |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Process** | Apply rules consistently to all submitters. | Block or delay proposals arbitrarily. |
| **Content** | Recommend precise edits when clarity is lacking. | Rewrite intent or substance without authorisation. |
| **Integrity** | Act with integrity, in accordance with the Cardano Constitution and in the best interests of the community. | Participate in this process as an editor in bad faith. |
| **Neutrality** | Organise discussion spaces and stay on-topic. Apply suggestions neutrally and without bias. | Use access to favour allies or disadvantage rivals. |
| **Transparency** | Maintain clear audit trails of all changes. | Change substance silently during format edits. |

## Protocol for Author-Authorised Edits

Authors retain full ownership of their proposals at all times.

- **Explicit Authorisation:** Editors may only perform direct edits when explicitly authorised by the author. This authorisation must specify the scope (e.g., formatting, metadata, or specific sections).
- **Durable Records:** All authorisations must be documented in a public, accessible location (e.g., PR comments, issue threads).
- **Identity Disclosure:** Editors shall transparently disclose their identity and a method of communication. Suggested edits made as a result of such communications shall be disclosed in the CAP process.
- **Right to Revoke:** Authors may revoke edit permissions at any time.
- **Default Path:** When in doubt, or in the absence of clear authorisation, always provide suggestions instead of direct edits.
- **Compilation of Minor Amendments:** With individual author consent, editors may suggest that a series of minor changes be compiled into a single constitutional amendment. If accepted, editors will make this compilation on a bi-annual basis (or as decided) and recommend that Intersect submit the relevant governance action.

## Governance & Lifecycle

### Editor Capacity

- **Minimum:** The active editor cohort must never fall below 3 members.
- **Maximum:** None. The team may scale as workload dictates.
- **Expansion:** Current editors may request the addition of new editors at any time.

### Eligibility

Editors must meet the following minimum criteria:

- **Constitutional Familiarity:** Well-versed in the Cardano Constitution (as amended), with an understanding of inter-clause dependencies, constitutionality requirements, and key governance mechanisms.
- **Comprehension:** Strong comprehension skills.
- **English Competency:** Fluent in English.
- **Track Record:** Demonstrated track record or qualifications supporting the above.

Editors may suggest changes to these criteria during the bootstrapping phase.

### Editor Selection

During the bootstrapping phase, the initial editors will develop a selection process for future editors and seek community input. This process shall require approval of the Civics Committee and must align with the principles of this document.

### The Bootstrap Cohort

- **Initial Formation:** 3 individuals, selected by the CAP Working Group and ratified by the Civics Committee.
- **Term:** Bootstrap Editors serve a one-year term, after which they must stand for a standard election to continue.
- **Review:** No later than 9 months into the bootstrapping phase, the cohort must review this document and propose any necessary amendments. These are subject to Civics Committee approval.

### Tenure & Removal

- **Tenure:** Editors serve indefinitely until they choose to resign.
- **Removal:** An Editor may be removed by majority vote of the active cohort, provided the remaining total does not drop below the minimum of 3. Grounds include sustained inactivity, abuse of authority, or severe process violations.
- **Vacancies:** Retired or removed positions must be filled through the standard election process within 90 days.

### Remuneration

Editors should publicly disclose any compensation received for fulfilling their role. Editors may request funding directly from the Cardano Treasury or similar funding vehicles for their work.

---

## Ethics & Accountability

Editors shall discharge their role ethically and in observance of the principles of this document — in particular the Operational Guidelines above.

Editors must remain impartial and accountable. If an Editor is an author or co-author of a specific CAP, or has a potential conflict of interest in relation to a proposal, they must **recuse themselves** from all procedural and administrative actions regarding that proposal.

Any community member may challenge process handling through established governance channels. Recurring non-compliance with these guidelines will trigger a formal removal review.
