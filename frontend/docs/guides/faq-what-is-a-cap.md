# What is a CAP?

A **Constitutional Amendment Proposal (CAP)** is a formal governance document that proposes a specific change, clarification, or addition to the Cardano Constitution or its governance processes.

---

## The short version

A CAP is how community members propose changes to the Cardano Constitution. It is submitted as a GitHub Issue, goes through a mandatory deliberation period, and — if accepted — forms the basis of an on-chain governance action to make the change official.

---

## What a CAP contains

Every CAP follows a defined structure:

- **Abstract** — a plain-language summary of what the proposal changes
- **Motivation** — why the change is needed; what problem it solves
- **Specification** — the exact change being proposed (a redline or replacement text)
- **Rationale** — why this specific approach was chosen over alternatives
- **Path to Ratification** — the on-chain governance action required to enact the change
- **Copyright** — always CC-BY-4.0

This structure ensures proposals are precise, justifiable, and actionable.

---

## What a CAP is not

- A CAP is **not** a petition or general opinion. It proposes specific constitutional text.
- A CAP does **not** itself enact any change. It is the off-chain preparation layer. Once ratified through the CAP process, a governance action must still be submitted on-chain.
- A CAP is **not** required before submitting a governance action on-chain. The CAP process exists to help proposals be well-formed and community-endorsed — it is not a gatekeeping mechanism.

---

## How it relates to a CIS

A **Constitutional Issue Statement (CIS)** defines a constitutional problem. A CAP proposes the fix. If you have spotted an ambiguity or governance gap but are unsure how to address it, a CIS is the right starting point.

See [Introduction to CAPs and CISs](intro-to-caps-and-cis) for the full picture.
