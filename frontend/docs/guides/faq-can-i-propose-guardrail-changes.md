# Can I propose changes to the Guardrails?

Yes. The Guardrails are part of the Constitution, and like any other constitutional provision they can be amended through the CAP process and ratified via an on-chain governance action.

---

## How to do it

Submit a CAP with:
- **Category: Technical** (for parameter value changes) or **Substantive** (for structural or policy changes to the Guardrails)
- A specification that identifies the specific Guardrail label(s) you are proposing to change, and the new text or values
- A rationale that explains why the change is needed and what problem it solves

Guardrail labels follow a naming convention (e.g. `TFPB-01`, `UCPB-02`) — use the exact label of the Guardrail you are addressing.

---

## Technical review requirement

The Constitution requires that Hard Fork Initiation and Parameter Update governance actions undergo "sufficient technical review and scrutiny as mandated by the Guardrails." If your proposal involves changing a parameter value, you should expect that reviewers will want evidence — benchmarking, simulation results, or performance analysis — to support the proposed change.

The CAP process does not itself mandate a specific form of technical review, but a proposal lacking technical justification is unlikely to gain broad community support or pass the on-chain vote.

---

## Guardrail-specific amendment thresholds

Some Guardrails have their own amendment threshold specified within the Guardrail itself. Where this is the case, that threshold applies instead of the general 65% constitutional amendment threshold. Check the specific Guardrail in Appendix I before drafting your Path to Ratification section.

---

## On-chain Guardrails Script implications

If the Guardrail you are proposing to change has been implemented as an on-chain Guardrails Script, changing the written Appendix alone is not sufficient — a governance action to replace or update the script will also be required. Note this in your Path to Ratification section.
