# Do I need a Cardano wallet?

It depends on what you want to do.

---

## You do NOT need a wallet to:

- Read any proposal — all proposals are publicly visible
- Read the Constitution in the [Constitution viewer](/#/constitution)
- Browse the CAP Portal, proposals list, and Learn Hub

---

## You DO need a Cardano wallet to:

- **Submit a CAP or CIS** — proposals require wallet authentication so your authorship is recorded on-chain
- **Comment on a proposal** — participating in deliberation requires you to be logged in
- **Signal readiness** — the author-ready signal requires sign-in

---

## Compatible wallets

The portal supports any wallet that implements CIP-30, including:

- **Eternl** — eternl.io
- **Lace** — lace.io
- **Nami** — namiwallet.io
- **Vespr**, **Typhon**, and other CIP-30 wallets

---

## Pseudonymous participation

You do not need to submit under your real name. Your identity on the portal is your Cardano stake address. You can set any display name you like during wallet connection. The CAP process has no identity verification requirement beyond wallet ownership.

If you are submitting a CAP and want credit under a specific name, use the display name field when connecting your wallet — it becomes the public record associated with your proposal.

---

## The portal and authentication

The CAP Portal can be used fully without authentication for reading. The **Amendment Wizard** — the guided proposal drafting tool — is available to logged-in users who connect their wallet.
