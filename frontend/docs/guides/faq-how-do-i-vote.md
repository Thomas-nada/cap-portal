# How do I vote?

The answer depends on what you mean by voting — there are two distinct levels: deliberation (off-chain) and governance actions (on-chain).

---

## During deliberation (off-chain)

The CAP process does not have a formal vote. Instead, community sentiment is expressed through:

- **Comments** on the GitHub Issue — the primary way to signal support, raise concerns, or suggest changes
- **Reactions** — use the 👍 👎 🎉 😕 buttons on the issue or individual comments to signal agreement or disagreement without writing a full response

CAP Editors read the discussion and reactions as part of their review. Strong community support or opposition will be reflected in how the proposal is handled.

This is deliberation, not a ballot. The goal is to produce a well-reasoned proposal, not to reach a binary yes/no count.

---

## On-chain governance votes

Once a proposal has been accepted through the CAP process (or submitted directly on-chain), the actual ratification vote happens on the Cardano Blockchain.

**Who votes:**

| Role | How |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Ada owners** | Delegate to a DRep who votes on their behalf, or register as a DRep themselves |
| **DReps** | Vote directly on on-chain governance actions, with weight proportional to delegated stake |
| **SPOs** | Vote on certain action types (Hard Fork, No Confidence, parameter changes, Info actions) |
| **Constitutional Committee** | Vote on whether governance actions are consistent with the Constitution |

**To participate in on-chain governance:**

1. Delegate your ada to a DRep whose views align with yours — or register as a DRep yourself
2. You can change your delegation at any time
3. You may delegate to yourself if you want to vote directly
4. If you use a custodian, you may authorise or withhold authorisation for them to vote on your behalf

---

## The Constitutional Committee is not a policy body

The CC does not vote on whether a proposal is good policy. It votes only on whether it is **consistent with the Constitution**. Policy judgments rest with DReps and SPOs.

---

## What threshold is needed?

For constitutional amendments, at least **65% of the active voting stake** must support the governance action. This is calculated from the total lovelace delegated to active DReps and SPOs (excluding stake delegated to the abstain option and other inactive categories).
