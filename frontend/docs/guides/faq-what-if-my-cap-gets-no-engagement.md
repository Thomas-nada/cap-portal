# What if my CAP gets no comments or engagement?

Low engagement during deliberation is not a rejection, and it does not prevent your proposal from advancing. But it is worth understanding what it means and how to respond.

---

## What low engagement means

The absence of comments is ambiguous. It could mean:

- The proposal is uncontroversial and people have no objection
- The proposal has not reached the right audience yet
- The proposal addresses a niche topic that fewer community members feel qualified to comment on
- The proposal has not been surfaced clearly enough

It does not mean the proposal is invalid or will be blocked.

---

## What an Editor will consider

When a CAP Editor reviews a proposal at the end of the deliberation period, they look at the quality and completeness of the proposal itself — not a comment count. A well-written, complete proposal with zero comments is still eligible to advance.

That said, if the proposal touches on something significant and community sentiment is unclear, an Editor may note this and encourage more outreach before moving the proposal to the Ready stage.

---

## What you can do

- **Post in relevant community channels** — Cardano Forum, Discord, X/Twitter, or other spaces where ada holders and DReps discuss governance
- **Tag relevant stakeholders** — if your proposal affects SPOs, DReps, or developers, make sure they are aware of it
- **Share a summary** — a short plain-language summary of what you are proposing and why can be more accessible than the full CAP text
- **Engage with the Editor** — if an Editor has reviewed and has no feedback, that is also a signal. You can ask directly whether they see any issues.

---

## If your proposal remains inactive

If you decide the proposal is no longer relevant or you want to revise and resubmit, you can withdraw it at any time using the Withdraw button in your Author Controls panel on the CAP Portal. This applies the `withdrawn` label and closes the issue — the closed issue remains as a permanent historical record. You can open a new issue with a revised version whenever you are ready.
