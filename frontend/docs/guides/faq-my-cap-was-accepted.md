# My CAP is Ready — what now?

Reaching the **Ready** stage means your proposal has cleared editorial review and is confirmed as community-endorsed. It is a significant milestone — but it is not the last step.

---

## What Ready means

When an editor moves your proposal to the Ready lifecycle stage, it means:

- The deliberation period is complete
- The proposal is well-formed and constitutionally sound
- You and the editor have both confirmed it is ready to proceed
- The proposal is now the community's formal position on this change

A permanent record of the proposal is committed to the repository.

---

## The on-chain step

To make the constitutional change official, a **governance action** must be submitted to the Cardano Blockchain. Your proposal contains a **Path to Ratification** section that specifies exactly what governance action is required.

You — or anyone else — may submit this governance action. The CAP process does not assign responsibility for on-chain submission to any particular party.

The governance action must be:
- Consistent with the CAP text
- In the standardised format required by the Constitution (URL + document hash + rationale)
- Approved by at least 65% of the active voting stake (or a different threshold if specified in the Guardrails for the specific provision being amended)

Once submitted on-chain, the editor will apply the `onchain` status tag to the proposal in the CAP Portal.

---

## Signalling readiness to close

Once the on-chain process is concluded, the same two-step confirmation applies:

1. **Signal Ready for Completion** via your Author Controls panel on the CAP Portal
2. The editor reviews and moves the proposal to **Done**

---

## What if no one submits the governance action?

A Ready proposal represents community will, but it has no mechanism to force an on-chain vote. If no governance action is submitted, the proposal remains in the Ready stage as a statement of community intent. Anyone may act on it at any time.

If you drafted the CAP and want to see it enacted, the most direct path is to submit the governance action yourself, or coordinate with DReps who support the change to do so.

---

## After the on-chain vote

If the governance action passes, the constitutional change is enacted on-chain. The repository will be updated to reflect the new version of the Constitution, and the amendment history will be updated accordingly. The proposal is moved to **Done**.

If the governance action fails, the proposal remains on record. A new CAP or a revised governance action may be submitted in the future.
