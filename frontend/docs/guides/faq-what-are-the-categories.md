# What CAP categories exist?

Every CAP and CIS must be assigned a category when submitted. The category determines the recommended minimum deliberation period and signals the nature of the change being proposed.

---

## The six categories

### Procedural
Changes to governance rules, processes, or administrative structures — how the CAP process itself works, how votes are conducted, how bodies operate.

**Minimum deliberation: 60 days**

Examples: changing the CAP submission format, modifying how CAP Editors are appointed, altering the process for CC elections.

---

### Substantive
Changes to the rights, obligations, or core principles in the Constitution — the substance of what the Constitution says and protects.

**Minimum deliberation: 60 days**

Examples: adding or removing a Tenet, changing ada owner rights, modifying the amendment threshold, altering the scope of the CC's role.

---

### Technical
Changes involving protocol parameters, technical specifications, or Guardrail values. These often require specialist review and external input.

**Minimum deliberation: Variable** — set on a case-by-case basis, as the appropriate review period depends on the technical scope and complexity of the change.

Examples: modifying a Guardrail value, proposing changes to on-chain parameter bounds, adjusting technical constraints in Appendix I.

---

### Interpretive
Proposals that clarify how existing constitutional text should be read, without changing its substance. These do not amend the Constitution itself — they establish a shared interpretation.

**Minimum deliberation: 30 days**

Examples: clarifying what "active voting stake" means in a specific context, resolving ambiguity about which bodies are bound by a particular provision.

---

### Editorial
Formatting, grammar, spelling, or structural corrections that do not affect the substantive meaning of any provision.

**Minimum deliberation: 14 days**

Examples: correcting a typo, standardising capitalisation, renumbering sections for consistency.

---

### Other
Proposals that do not fit neatly into the above categories.

**Minimum deliberation: 30 days**

If you are unsure which category applies, start with Other and note the ambiguity in your proposal — a CAP Editor can help clarify during review.

---

## Choosing the right category

When in doubt, err toward the longer deliberation period. Categorising a Substantive change as Editorial — intentionally or not — may be flagged by an Editor during initial review.

If your proposal touches multiple categories (for example, a Substantive change with Technical implications), choose the most significant one.
