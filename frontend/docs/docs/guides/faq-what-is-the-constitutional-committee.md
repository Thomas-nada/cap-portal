# What is the Constitutional Committee?

The **Constitutional Committee (CC)** is one of the three governance bodies on the Cardano Blockchain. Its sole function is to assess whether proposed governance actions are consistent with the Constitution.

---

## What it does

Before most governance actions can be enacted on-chain, a requisite percentage of CC members must affirm that the action does not violate the Constitution. Without this affirmation, the action cannot proceed.

The CC is **strictly limited** to constitutionality review. It does not assess whether a governance action is good policy, economically sound, or desirable for the community — only whether it is consistent with the Constitution.

When a CC member votes that a governance action is unconstitutional, they must individually state the specific Articles or Guardrail provisions that are in conflict. This is a mandatory requirement — the collective body cannot issue a generic objection without individual reasoning.

---

## What it cannot do

- The CC cannot block **No Confidence** or **Update Committee** actions
- The CC cannot prevent **Info** actions from being recorded on-chain (though members may record their constitutional opinion on an Info action)
- The CC cannot evaluate policy merits — only constitutionality

---

## Composition and terms

The CC is composed of a number of members determined by ada owners, serving staggered terms to ensure continuity. The minimum and maximum term lengths and membership sizes are set in the Guardrails Appendix.

---

## States of the CC

The CC is always in one of two states:

- **Confidence** — the CC is functioning normally; governance proceeds
- **No Confidence** — the CC has been removed or has lost confidence; no governance actions except Info actions may proceed until the CC is reinstated or replaced via an Update Committee action

---

## Relationship to CAPs

CAPs are not submitted to the CC. The CC only acts on on-chain governance actions. However, a CAP that is constitutionally problematic — identified during the off-chain deliberation process — is unlikely to reach the on-chain stage without being revised.
