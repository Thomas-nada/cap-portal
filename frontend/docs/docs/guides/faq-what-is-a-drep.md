# What is a DRep?

A **Delegated Representative (DRep)** is an individual or entity registered to vote on on-chain governance actions — either on their own behalf or on behalf of other ada owners who have delegated their voting stake to them.

---

## How DReps work

Ada owners cannot vote on governance actions directly as individual stakeholders. Instead, they delegate their voting stake to a DRep, and that DRep votes on their behalf. The DRep's voting power is proportional to the total lovelace delegated to them.

Any ada owner may register as a DRep. If you register as a DRep and delegate to yourself, you vote directly.

---

## Delegating to a DRep

- You may delegate to one or more registered DReps
- You may change your delegation at any time
- If you use a custodian to hold your ada, you may authorise or withhold authorisation for that custodian to delegate on your behalf

There are also two predefined voting options that function like DReps:
- **Abstain** — your stake is excluded from the active voting stake calculation (it does not count toward or against any threshold)
- **No Confidence** — your stake is permanently cast as a no confidence vote on the CC

---

## DRep obligations

DReps must:
- **Publicly disclose** any compensation received in connection with their DRep activities, in a timely manner through relevant governance channels
- **Not offer or provide compensation** to ada owners in exchange for being appointed as their DRep or for voting on their behalf

---

## DReps and CAPs

DReps are not involved in the CAP process — that is an off-chain deliberation mechanism. DReps vote on on-chain governance actions, which is the step that follows (and is distinct from) CAP acceptance.

If you are an ada owner who wants to influence how governance actions are voted on, selecting a DRep whose views align with yours is how you participate in on-chain voting.
