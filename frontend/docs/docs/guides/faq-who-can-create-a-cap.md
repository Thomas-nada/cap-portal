# Who can create a CAP?

Anyone. There are no eligibility requirements, credentials, or approval gates for submitting a CAP or CIS.

---

## What you need

The only practical requirement is a **GitHub account**, which is free to create at [github.com](https://github.com). The GitHub account is needed to submit an issue, comment during deliberation, and respond to editor feedback.

You do not need to be:

- An ada holder
- A DRep, SPO, or CC member
- A developer or technical expert
- An established member of the Cardano community

---

## No permission required

The CAP process is open by design. The Cardano Constitution establishes that any ada owner may participate in governance, and the CAP process reflects this: anyone who wants to propose a change or document a problem can do so.

CAP Editors review proposals for completeness and constitutionality — but they do not decide who is allowed to submit.

---

## Authors retain full ownership

Once you submit a CAP, it is yours. Editors may ask questions, suggest edits, and flag concerns, but they cannot modify your proposal or remove you from the process. You can update the proposal, respond to feedback, or withdraw it at any time.

If you disagree with the CAP process entirely, you may also bypass it and submit a governance action on-chain directly — the CAP process is not a gatekeeping mechanism.

---

## Where to start

The [Amendment Wizard](/#/wizard) in the CAP Portal guides you through drafting a proposal step by step, including selecting the exact constitutional text you want to change.

Alternatively, see [Creating a CAP or CIS via GitHub](creating-a-cap) for the manual submission process.
